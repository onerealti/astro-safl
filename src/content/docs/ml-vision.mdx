## 1. Embedded Compute & Vision Configuration

The machine learning system is deployed directly at the edge to enable real-time detection without depending on cloud processing.

<div class="mermaid">{`
flowchart LR
    RealSense["Intel RealSense D435"] -->|USB 3.0 RGB-D| Jetson["NVIDIA Jetson Nano<br>Ubuntu 18.04 + PyTorch"]
    Jetson -->|UART Commands| Arduino["Arduino Due<br>Low-Level Control"]
    AmiciProbe["Amici Sense Soil Probe"] -->|UART Serial Telemetry| Jetson
    Jetson -->|Logging| CSV["Local Data Log CSV"]

*   **Central Processor**: **NVIDIA Jetson Nano** running Ubuntu 18.04 LTS (JetPack SDK)
*   **Deep Learning Backend**: PyTorch
*   **Primary Perception Sensor**: **Intel RealSense D435** depth camera
    *   **Data Channels**: RGB frames fused with Active Infrared Stereo depth maps
    *   **Interface**: USB 3.0
    *   **Mounting Alignment**: Centered on chassis axis, pitched downward at **10°** (optimized from 15° to cover near-field crop zone between the front wheels)

## 3. Vibration & Jitter Mitigation

To prevent camera shake from causing image blur and ML misclassifications, the robot uses a dual mechanical-software stabilization strategy:

### Mechanical Countermeasures

<CardGrid>
  <Card title="Vibration Isolators" icon="setting">
    Integrated rubber washers on all camera bracket bolt threads to absorb high-frequency chassis rattle.
  </Card>
  <Card title="Damping Spacers" icon="setting">
    High-density foam inserts situated underneath the battery pack and Jetson base plate to dampen low-frequency bumps.
  </Card>
  <Card title="Balance Counterweights" icon="setting">
    Geometrically balanced motor shaft mounts to minimize rotational resonance during locomotion.
  </Card>
</CardGrid>

### Software Countermeasures

<CardGrid>
  <Card title="RealSense Frame Averaging" icon="laptop">
    Averages consecutive depth frames to eliminate high-frequency visual and depth noise.
  </Card>
  <Card title="Confidence Filtering" icon="laptop">
    Computes a **3-frame rolling average** before confirming and executing weed bounding box triggers.
  </Card>
  <Card title="Sensor Telemetry Sync" icon="laptop">
    Halts locomotion motors during soil probe insertion to completely eliminate vibration noise during telemetry transmission.
  </Card>
</CardGrid>

### Actuation & Low-Level Control Architecture

Precise and coordinated joint movements are managed using a dual-controller layout:
*   **Low-Level Controller**: An **Arduino Due** acts as the dedicated real-time actuator controller (84 MHz clock frequency, 12 hardware PWM outputs, and multiple hardware serial UART interfaces).
*   **High-Level Interface**: Commands are received from the NVIDIA Jetson Nano over a dedicated serial connection running at **115,200 baud**. The Arduino Due parses these motion paths and translates them into PWM commands.
*   **Actuators & Drivers**:
    *   **Geared DC Motors**: Four planetary geared units for shoulder articulation (driven via dual H-bridge motor drivers).
    *   **Brushless Hub Motors**: Two distal direct-drive hub motors (driven via high-current Electronic Speed Controllers or ESCs) provide primary locomotion.

### Power Rail Separation

To prevent motor current draws from causing brownouts or logic resets on the Jetson Nano, power rails are split:
*   **Logic Rail**: 5V rail stepped down from the main 24V bus via a DC-DC buck converter to supply the Jetson Nano, camera, and sensors.
*   **Geared Motor Rail**: A regulated 12V supply bus managed by the H-bridge drivers for shoulder articulation.
*   **Locomotion Motor Rail**: A direct 24V high-current bus from the battery pack to the ESCs for the hub wheels.

### EMI Shielding & Signal Isolation

To prevent electromagnetic interference (EMI) from the high-torque actuators from corrupting the low-voltage sensor lines:
*   **Perimeter Routing**: High-current motor lines are routed strictly along the outer edges of the chassis frame.
*   **Central Data Channels**: Low-voltage signal lines are elevated and routed centrally.
*   **Ferrite Suppression**: Ferrite beads are added to motor cable pairs, and all motor logic wires are shielded and grounded to establish common reference planes.
